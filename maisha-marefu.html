Who They Are

Maisha Marefu was founded in 2022 by Louise Soubrier and Sergio Caldeira, visionary leaders committed to transforming lives in East Africa. Their mission centers on creating sustainable pathways out of poverty through regenerative agriculture, food security, and community empowerment.

Built on the principle that every individual deserves dignity and opportunity, Maisha Marefu works hand-in-hand with communities to build resilience, create livelihoods, and foster sustainable development from the ground up.

"The greatest gift is not what we give, but what we enable others to create for themselves."

— Maisha Marefu Foundation

Community Impact

Four Core Pillars

Our multifaceted approach to sustainable community development

🌱

Regenerative Agriculture

Building soil health and environmental resilience through sustainable farming practices that restore ecosystems while producing nutritious food for communities.

🥗

Food Security

Ensuring families have consistent access to nutritious, locally-grown food. Programs address malnutrition and dietary diversity while reducing dependence on imported goods.

💼

Sustainable Livelihoods

Creating economic opportunities through training, skills development, and market access. Empowering individuals to generate stable income and build financial independence.

👩‍💼

Women's Entrepreneurship

Prioritizing women's leadership and business creation. Providing mentorship, capital access, and networks to unlock entrepreneurial potential and drive community-wide prosperity.

How We Work

Our proven five-step methodology for lasting impact

1

Community Assessment

Deep engagement with local communities to understand needs, assets, and existing knowledge. We listen, learn, and build trust before implementing any program.

2

Participatory Planning

Communities and Maisha Marefu partners co-design solutions. Local stakeholders lead decision-making, ensuring programs reflect their priorities and cultural context.

3

Implementation & Training

Hands-on training in regenerative agriculture, business skills, and food production. We provide technical support, resources, and mentorship throughout the learning process.

4

Market Access & Linkages

Connecting farmers and entrepreneurs to buyers, markets, and networks. Building supply chains and business relationships that enable economic growth and sustainability.

5

Monitoring & Adaptation

Continuous feedback and measurement of impact. We adapt strategies based on community feedback, market conditions, and outcomes to ensure lasting, meaningful change.

Our Connection with Lorubae

Lorubae Comprehensive School serves as a vital hub and partner for Maisha Marefu's initiatives in Samburu East. Through collaborative programming, our students gain practical experience in sustainable agriculture, entrepreneurship, and community development while directly contributing to the well-being of their region.

This partnership bridges education with action—students learn not just in classrooms but through real-world engagement with regenerative farming, food production, and livelihood-building projects. The school becomes a center of innovation and leadership training, equipping the next generation with skills to transform their communities.

Together, Lorubae and Maisha Marefu are cultivating leaders who understand that true development comes not from external rescue, but from communities recognizing their own power to create lasting change.
